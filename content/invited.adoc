Her main research interests are in computational pragmatics, building models that capture what people infer "between the lines". Her 2012 dissertation highlighted the need to capture variation in human annotations. She is also one of the developers of the Universal Dependencies framework. Her research work has been funded by Google Inc., the National Science Foundation and the FNRS.</p>
